# Visual Review Notes

The live preview successfully loads the Assurances Cardone page with the official black-and-gold branding, dark theme, navigation, and hero content. The header, metallic gold CTA, and vault-like hero background are rendering.

A notable visual issue is present in the hero: the uploaded logo is displayed inside a large tall rectangular card with a black background, making it feel overly dominant and somewhat poster-like rather than integrated into the hero composition. The logo itself is correct, but its container likely needs refinement in size, cropping, or presentation.

The quote application is already visible in the initial viewport and appears structurally functional with step-one fields. Further review is still needed for lower sections, including the legacy bento grid, service flip cards, and footer.

Additional review confirms that the hero typography, command-deck panel, and gold CTA styling are rendering strongly, with a premium dark atmosphere and convincing glassmorphic treatment on the right-side information card.

The legacy section also renders well: the large serif heading, asymmetrical composition, and bento cards feel aligned with the Digital Vault direction. The main remaining concern from the current review is still the hero logo treatment, which appears inside a very tall dark rectangle and visually overpowers the more elegant surrounding composition.

Bilingual validation succeeded in the live preview. The site now loads in French by default, matching the Montreal/Quebec requirement, and the FR/EN toggle successfully switches the visible interface copy to English.

A remaining presentation issue persists in the hero area: the official logo file contains a solid black rectangular background, so it still reads as a large dark poster over the hero rather than a fully integrated mark. This does not block the language-toggle feature, but it remains the main polish issue visible in the current build.
