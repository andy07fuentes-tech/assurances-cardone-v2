# Research notes — broker rate integrations

## Source set 1

### Applied Rating Services
- URL: https://www1.appliedsystems.com/en-ca/solutions/for-brokers/insurer-connectivity/applied-rating-services/
- Applied describes Applied Rating Services as Canada’s comparative rating software for brokerages.
- The product page says it provides accurate pricing and rate comparisons for automobile and property risks.
- The page states that it integrates with the brokerage management system so customer and prospect information can be prefilled instead of re-entered in another application.
- The listed capabilities include auto and property rating automation, data validation, data prefill, sales reporting, and management-system integration.
- The page also notes self-service quoting support.

### QuickFacts
- URL: https://www.quickfactsinc.com/
- QuickFacts presents itself as a broker productivity and underwriting-comparison platform.
- The homepage emphasizes quick access to operating information in one place and says the tool can save brokers time each day.
- The feature list centers on comparing carrier information such as wordings, contacts, binding guidelines, bulletins, decline rules, manuals, forms, and payment resources.
- Based on the homepage, QuickFacts appears oriented toward underwriting/comparison workflow support rather than exposing a public consumer quote API.

## Source set 2

### QuickFacts Québec launch
QuickFacts' official news page says the company launched its personal lines offering in Québec in March 2026. The page describes QuickFacts as a tool for brokers, built by brokers, and says it brings comparative rating to the product level. The same news feed also highlights integrations and collaborations aimed at helping brokers search, compare, and place risks more confidently in one centralized location.

### CSIO certification for Applied Systems Canada
CSIO states that Applied Systems Canada became the first broker management system vendor to achieve CSIO's API Security Standards Certification and Platinum Standards Certification Rating in June 2025. CSIO explains that its API Security Standards are authentication and authorization guidelines, and says Applied demonstrated that customer information exchanged between Applied TAM or Applied Epic and insurer systems is protected by leading security safeguards. CSIO also explicitly links this standards posture to benefits for brokers including heightened security, real-time quoting, and more efficient work processes.

## Source set 3

### Applied ecosystem and connectivity posture
Applied's Canadian ecosystem page says the company uses an open approach to integrations, supports customers in integrations that extend the value of their core technology stack, and works with third parties to embed capabilities natively in brokerage workflows where there is market need. The page also says Applied has more than 20 certified integrations, more than 100 custom and pre-built integrations, and more than 100,000 users leveraging integrations.

### Applied insurer-connectivity positioning
Applied's Canadian insurer-connectivity overview says the purpose of insurer connectivity is to help brokerages connect to insurers, access more products, automate policy information exchange, and enable faster service plus self-service quoting. The page also cites an Applied brokerage connectivity survey and positions connectivity as a practical business enabler rather than only a technical feature.
