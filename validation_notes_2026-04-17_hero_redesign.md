# Lemonade-inspired hero validation

The Lemonade homepage reference was reviewed directly before implementation. The hero effect appears to rely on a clean centered editorial composition with layered illustration and depth cues, rather than a literal embedded 3D video or canvas scene.

The Cardone hero was redesigned to adapt that structure into the existing black-and-gold brand language. The updated live preview now uses a centered headline zone with staged faux-3D side panels, a floating monogram marker, and stronger depth framing around the copy.

The redesign preserves unobstructed primary and secondary calls to action, keeps the bilingual navigation intact, and continues to compile successfully in production. The only build note remains the existing non-blocking bundle-size warning.

## Bright looping hero video validation

The faux-3D side panels were replaced with a real looping hero video showing a brighter peace-of-mind scene: an upscale residence, a premium vehicle, a seated person, and a dog in warm daylight. The live preview confirms that the motion now reads as a full-background lifestyle tableau rather than a dark vault effect.

After refinement, the hero uses a stronger glass-like central scrim to keep the headline and calls to action readable over the lighter video. The result feels warmer, more reassuring, and closer to the calm staged clarity requested from the Lemonade reference while still preserving Cardone’s premium tone.

The updated site continues to pass production build successfully. The remaining build note is the existing non-blocking bundle-size warning.
